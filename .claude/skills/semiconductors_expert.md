## The Semiconductor Cycle Framework

### The Cycle (This Is Everything)

Semiconductors are the most cyclical sector in tech. Understanding where you are in the cycle is 80% of the trade:

**Phase 1: Downturn (6-12 months)**
- Inventories building at customers → order cuts → utilization drops
- Memory prices falling → negative margin revisions
- Equipment orders declining → capex cuts announced
- **This is when you BUY.** The market extrapolates the downturn forever

**Phase 2: Trough (3-6 months)**
- Inventory correction ending → channel depletion
- Memory prices stabilizing → contract prices bottom
- Equipment bookings at minimum → new orders trickling in
- **Peak pessimism.** Stocks bottom 1-2 quarters before earnings trough

**Phase 3: Recovery (6-12 months)**
- Restocking demand → orders accelerating
- Memory prices rising → ASP inflection drives margin expansion
- Equipment lead times extending → capacity constraints emerging
- **Sweet spot for semi stocks.** Earnings revisions up + multiple expansion

**Phase 4: Peak (6-12 months)**
- Double-ordering → channel stuffing risk
- Memory prices at highs → unsustainable margins
- Equipment backlogs extended → everyone adding capacity simultaneously
- **This is when you SELL.** Maximum bullishness = maximum risk

### Key Cycle Indicators (In Order of Leading to Lagging)

1. **DRAM/NAND spot prices** (most leading) — Check DRAMeXchange weekly
2. **Equipment bookings** (ASML quarterly orders) — Leading indicator for future supply
3. **Distribution inventory** (Avnet, Arrow reports) — Channel health
4. **Foundry utilization rates** — TSMC monthly revenue as proxy
5. **Lead times** — Track via earnings calls, purchasing manager surveys
6. **Earnings revisions** — Most lagging, by the time analysts catch up the move is half done

### Memory Deep Dive (MU, Samsung, SK Hynix)

Memory is the purest cycle play in semis:

**DRAM Economics:**
- 3 players: Samsung (~40%), SK Hynix (~30%), Micron (~25%)
- Tight oligopoly = rational pricing when demand strong
- HBM (High Bandwidth Memory) is the premium tier: 3-5x ASP vs commodity DDR5
- HBM supply allocated 4+ quarters out. SK Hynix leads (NVDA qualified), Samsung catching up

**NAND Economics:**
- More fragmented = more competitive = worse pricing power
- Oversupply cycles more severe and longer
- 3D NAND layer count competition (200+ layers) driving cost reductions

**Key signal**: When DRAM contract prices rise for 2+ consecutive quarters AND spot-contract spread narrows, the memory upcycle has legs. History says memory stocks rally 50-100% in upcycles.

### Equipment Deep Dive (ASML, AMAT, LRCX, KLAC)

Equipment is LEADING — they see the capex plans before anyone else:

**ASML (Monopoly — EUV)**
- Only company making EUV lithography systems ($150M+ each)
- Order book = future wafer fab capacity. Backlog duration = demand visibility
- High-NA EUV ($350M+) for 2nm and below — next upgrade cycle
- **Key signal**: ASML quarterly orders. Rising orders = fabs investing = cycle turning up

**AMAT, LRCX, KLAC (Oligopoly)**
- Each dominant in their niche (deposition, etch, inspection)
- Equipment stocks bottom 1-2 quarters BEFORE semiconductor stocks
- When capex cuts stop, equipment stocks rally first (leveraged to new orders)

### China Risk/Opportunity

Export controls create winners and losers:

**Winners:** ASML (can't be replicated), domestic China equipment plays
**Losers:** AMAT, LRCX, KLAC (lost 25-30% of China revenue)
**Complex:** NVDA (A800 compliant chips vs full H100 blacklisted)

**Key signal**: Each new export control round → short-term negative for equipment, medium-term bullish (accelerates Western reshoring capex, reduces future China supply competition).

## Earnings Call Intelligence

**What to listen for:**
1. "Lead times" — extending = demand strong, contracting = weakness ahead
2. "Inventory" — "lean" at customers = restocking imminent, "elevated" = risk
3. "Utilization" — below 80% = margin pressure, above 90% = pricing power
4. "Design wins" — quantity and quality of new customer engagements
5. "China" revenue % — declining share = policy impact priced in?

## Risk Factors
- Cycle timing: being 1 quarter early can mean 20% drawdown before the turn
- Geopolitical: Taiwan Strait escalation = existential risk for TSMC-dependent names
- AI demand masking: strong AI chip demand can hide weakness in consumer/auto/industrial
