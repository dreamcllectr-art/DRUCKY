## The Defense Investment Framework

### The Budget Cycle (Foundation of Everything)

Defense spending is the most predictable revenue stream in any sector — IF you understand the cycle:

**Budget Process Timeline:**
- February: President's Budget Request (PBR) submitted
- March-September: Congressional markups, debates, amendments
- October 1: New fiscal year begins (often with Continuing Resolution)
- Actual appropriation often delayed to December-March (CR extensions)

**Key insight**: The NDAA (National Defense Authorization Act) authorizes programs. Appropriations bills fund them. Authorization without appropriation = delayed revenue. Watch BOTH.

**Current structural drivers:**
- Bipartisan support for defense spending (rare in Washington)
- Peer competition (China, Russia) drives long-cycle programs
- 3-5% real growth trajectory for foreseeable future
- Supplemental spending (Ukraine, Israel, Indo-Pacific) on top of base budget

### Sub-Sector Ranking

**Tier 1: Multi-Decade Programs (Highest Visibility)**
1. **Strategic Deterrence**: Sentinel ICBM (NOC), Columbia-class submarine (GD/HII), B-21 (NOC) — $200B+ combined, 20+ year production runs
2. **Space**: Proliferated LEO constellation (LMT, NOC, L3Harris), missile tracking (RTX)
3. **Hypersonics**: Dark Eagle (LMT), HACM (RTX) — scramjet tech with growing budgets

**Tier 2: High-Growth Segments**
4. **Munitions replenishment**: GMLRS, JDAM, Javelin, Stinger — Ukraine/Israel depleted stocks, multi-year replenishment orders (RTX, LMT, GD)
5. **Cyber/Electronic Warfare**: Fastest-growing budget line. CACI, BAH, LHX, Palantir
6. **Autonomous/AI**: Replicator initiative — thousands of low-cost autonomous systems. L3Harris, AeroVironment, Kratos

**Tier 3: Recovery/Turnaround**
7. **Commercial Aerospace** (BA, SPR, TDG, HEI): Narrow-body recovery, wide-body ramp. BA quality issues create uncertainty but production MUST ramp
8. **Aftermarket** (TDG, HEI): Sole-source monopolies on aircraft parts. 20-30% margins, recurring revenue

### Backlog Analysis (The Key Metric)

Defense companies are valued on backlog, not just current revenue:

**What to measure:**
- **Funded backlog**: Money appropriated and under contract — highest certainty
- **Unfunded backlog**: Contract signed but awaiting annual appropriation
- **Book-to-bill ratio**: >1.0x = growing backlog = accelerating revenue ahead
- **Backlog duration**: Revenue visibility in years (4-5 years = exceptional)

**Key signal**: When a defense company's backlog/revenue ratio expands for 2+ consecutive quarters AND book-to-bill is >1.1x, the revenue growth is durable and the stock re-rates.

### Margin Expansion Opportunity

The biggest mispriced dynamic in defense:

**Development → Production Transition:**
- Development phase (LRIP): Cost-plus contracts, low margins (5-8%)
- Full-rate production (FRP): Fixed-price contracts, learning curve drives margins to 12-15%
- This transition happens gradually over 2-4 years and is often UNDERESTIMATED by the market

**Key programs approaching FRP:**
- F-35 (LMT): Tech Refresh 3 and Block 4 upgrades → higher per-unit margins
- Sentinel ICBM (NOC): Engineering & Manufacturing Development → production in late 2020s
- CH-53K (LMT): Moving from LRIP to FRP → margin inflection

### International Sales (Underappreciated)

Foreign Military Sales (FMS) are a secular growth driver:

- NATO 2% GDP commitment: Most European nations still below target
- European rearmament: Post-Ukraine, defense budgets surging (Germany +€100B fund)
- Indo-Pacific allies: Japan, South Korea, Australia, India increasing procurement
- FMS pipeline: $80B+ pending, typically 2-4 year lead time from letter of intent to delivery

**Key signal**: When a US defense company wins a foreign competition (F-35 to Poland, THAAD to Saudi Arabia), the follow-on orders and sustainment contracts extend revenue for 20+ years.

## Earnings Call Intelligence

**What to listen for:**
1. "Book-to-bill" — the single most important metric
2. "Backlog" — total, funded vs unfunded, duration
3. "Margin rate" guidance — development vs production mix shift
4. "International" pipeline — new campaigns, down-selects, LOIs
5. "Supply chain" — specific bottlenecks (solid rocket motors, microelectronics, titanium)
6. "Workforce" — hiring challenges = demand signal but execution risk

## Risk Factors
- Budget sequestration/CR risk: Disrupts new starts, not existing programs
- Cost overruns: Fixed-price development contracts (like Sentinel early phase) can destroy margins
- Boeing execution: Quality/safety issues create persistent overhang on the entire sector
- Political risk: Changes in administration can shift priorities (but rarely cut total spending)
