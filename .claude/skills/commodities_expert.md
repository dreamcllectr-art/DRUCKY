## The Commodities Investment Framework

### Copper — The Most Important Industrial Metal

Copper tells you more about the global economy than any financial indicator:

**Demand side:**
- China = 50%+ of global consumption (construction, manufacturing, grid)
- Electrification supercycle: EVs use 4x more copper than ICE vehicles (80kg vs 20kg)
- AI data centers: each GW of power capacity = 4,000-5,000 tonnes of copper
- Grid buildout: renewable integration requires massive copper for transmission

**Supply side:**
- No major new mines discovered in 10+ years. Existing mines degrading (lower ore grades)
- Permitting timeline: 10-15 years from discovery to first production
- Chile (30% of global supply): water constraints, labor issues, royalty increases
- Congo/Zambia: geopolitical risk, infrastructure bottlenecks

**Key signals:**
- LME warehouse inventory: below 200K tonnes = very tight
- COMEX net speculative positioning: extreme shorts = squeeze potential
- China bonded warehouse inventory: real-time demand indicator
- TC/RC (treatment/refining charges): falling = tight concentrate market

**Key insight:** The copper market is heading toward a structural deficit of 5-10 million tonnes by 2030. Current prices don't reflect this — the supply gap has no solution at any price on a 5-year horizon.

### Gold — The Anti-Fragile Asset

**What actually drives gold (forget the narratives):**
1. Real rates (10Y TIPS yield) — MOST important. Falling real rates = gold up
2. Central bank buying — structural shift: $1T+ in reserves diversifying from USD
3. DXY (inverse) — weaker dollar = gold up
4. Geopolitical risk premium — tail risk hedge

**Current structural drivers:**
- Central bank buying at record pace (China, India, Turkey, Poland)
- De-dollarization trend: BRICS+ settling more trade in local currencies
- Fiscal dominance: US debt/GDP trajectory makes gold attractive as reserve
- Gold works in BOTH rate-cutting AND crisis environments

**Key signal:** When gold breaks to new all-time highs on NO news = institutional accumulation. When it rallies with stocks = inflation hedge bid. When it rallies against stocks = safe haven bid.

### Agricultural Commodities

**What moves ag prices:**
1. Weather — #1 driver. Droughts, floods, freezes during planting/growing season
2. USDA WASDE reports — monthly supply/demand estimates. Stocks-to-use ratio is the key metric
3. Export dynamics — Black Sea grain corridor, China import behavior
4. Fertilizer costs — MOS, CF, NTR margins correlate with grain prices (higher grain = farmers buy more fertilizer)

**Key crops:**
- Corn: Ethanol demand (40% of US corn), feed demand, weather sensitivity
- Soybeans: China import demand, crushing margins, biodiesel
- Wheat: Geopolitical supply risk (Russia/Ukraine = 30% of global exports)

**Key signal:** When USDA crop condition ratings fall below 5-year average by >10pp AND stocks-to-use ratio is below 10%, prices rally 20-40%.

### Industrial Metals Cycle

**Iron Ore / Steel:**
- China property sector = 30% of steel demand. Policy shifts move iron ore 20-30%
- Blast furnace utilization rates: above 80% = restocking demand
- Australian/Brazilian export discipline: Vale/BHP/RIO production guidance

**Aluminum:**
- Most energy-intensive metal. Power costs = 40% of production cost
- China smelter curtailments (environmental policy) = instant supply reduction
- EV/lightweighting demand structural growth

**Lithium:**
- Boom/bust cycle. Currently oversupplied → prices down 80% from peak
- High-cost producers shutting down. Next deficit cycle = violent price recovery
- Watch: EV adoption rate vs mining supply response

### The Supercycle Debate

Evidence FOR a new commodities supercycle:
- Chronic underinvestment in new supply (2015-2020 capex trough)
- Electrification demand: copper, aluminum, lithium, nickel, rare earths
- AI power demand: additive to existing grid requirements
- Inventory-to-consumption ratios at multi-decade lows for several metals

Evidence AGAINST:
- China property structural decline (20-30% of global metals demand)
- Substitution and efficiency improvements
- Recycling increasing as secondary supply source

**Key insight:** Even without a supercycle, the supply constraint alone creates 3-5 year upside for copper, gold, and uranium.

## Earnings Call Intelligence

**What to listen for:**
1. "Ore grades" — declining grades = higher costs per tonne = tighter supply
2. "Capex guidance" — maintenance only vs growth capex. Discipline = supply constraint
3. "Inventory" — company and customer inventory levels
4. "Production guidance" — downgrades = supply miss. Upgrades rare
5. "Cost curve position" — where they sit vs marginal producer. Low-cost wins in downturns

## Risk Factors
- China policy shifts can move commodity prices 20-30% in weeks
- Currency risk: commodity prices in USD, many producers in AUD/BRL/ZAR
- Geopolitical: resource nationalism, export bans, sanctions
- Substitution: too-high prices accelerate alternatives (copper → aluminum, etc.)
