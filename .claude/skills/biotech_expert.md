## The Biotech Investment Framework

### Layer 1: Pipeline Catalysts (Highest Alpha)

The single biggest driver of biotech returns is clinical data readouts and FDA decisions. These are binary events that the market systematically misprices:

**FDA Decision Types (by predictability):**
- **Standard Review (10-month PDUFA)**: Market usually prices in ~70% approval probability. Actual approval rate for standard reviews with positive Phase 3 data: ~85-90%
- **Priority Review (6-month PDUFA)**: Higher approval probability (~90%+). Market discount often excessive
- **Breakthrough Therapy**: Fastest pathway. ~80% of BTD-designated drugs get approved. Market still underweights
- **Accelerated Approval**: Based on surrogate endpoints. Approval likely but post-marketing risk real
- **AdCom Meetings**: Market overreacts to negative AdCom votes. FDA disagrees with AdCom ~25% of the time

**Key signal**: When a drug has Phase 3 data that met its primary endpoint, Breakthrough Therapy Designation, AND an upcoming PDUFA — the approval probability is >90% but the stock often prices in only 60-70%.

### Layer 2: Patent Cliffs & Biosimilars

The most predictable negative catalyst in pharma:

**Patent Cliff Timeline Rules:**
- Revenue erosion typically begins 12-18 months BEFORE patent expiry (market front-runs)
- Small molecule generics: 80-90% revenue erosion in 12-24 months
- Biologic biosimilars: SLOWER erosion — 30-50% over 3-5 years (more complex to manufacture, doctor switching inertia)
- Market consensus OVERESTIMATES biosimilar erosion speed for complex biologics

**Current Major Cliffs to Watch:**
- Humira (ABBV): Already losing share but biosimilar uptake slower than feared
- Keytruda (MRK): Patent expires 2028 — biggest cliff in pharma history ($25B+ drug)
- Eliquis (BMY/PFE): Generic competition beginning 2026-2028
- Stelara (JNJ): Biosimilar entry 2025 — watch erosion rate vs consensus

**Key signal**: When a stock is down 30%+ purely on patent cliff fears with 3+ years until expiry, the pipeline optionality is often being given zero value.

### Layer 3: Rare Disease & Orphan Drugs

The most durable moat in pharma:

- Orphan Drug Exclusivity: 7 years in US, 10 in EU — no generic competition
- Small patient populations = high per-patient pricing ($200K-$500K/year)
- Market underestimates commercial ramp in rare disease (diagnosis improvement = growing TAM)
- Key players: VRTX (CF franchise), REGN, BMRN, ALNY, IONS

**Key signal**: Rare disease drug with expanding label (new indications, younger patient populations) often has 10+ year revenue runway that DCF models undercount.

### Layer 4: GLP-1 Revolution

The biggest therapeutic class disruption in decades:

- LLY (Mounjaro/Zepbound) and NVO (Ozempic/Wegovy) dominating
- Total addressable market: $100B+ (obesity, diabetes, NASH, CKD, CVD)
- Supply constraints create pricing power + demand visibility
- Second-order effects: bearish for medical device companies (bariatric surgery), insulin pumps, sleep apnea devices

## Earnings Call Intelligence

**What to listen for in biotech earnings:**
1. Pipeline enrollment rates — "ahead of schedule" vs "enrollment challenges"
2. Data readout timing specificity — vague = delayed, specific date = confidence
3. Regulatory interaction language — "Type B meeting" with FDA = substantive, "pre-IND" = early
4. Manufacturing scale-up commentary — investment in CMO capacity = confidence in approval
5. Cash position vs runway — <18 months cash = dilution risk, likely secondary offering

## Risk Factors
- Clinical trial failure rate: ~90% from Phase 1 to approval. Never go all-in on single-drug biotechs
- Political/pricing risk: drug pricing reform rhetoric spikes in election years
- Short squeeze dynamics: heavily shorted small-cap biotechs can move 50%+ on positive data
