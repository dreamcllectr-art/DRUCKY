## The REIT Investment Framework

### The Rate Regime Is Everything

REITs are first and foremost rate-sensitive instruments. The 10Y Treasury yield drives cap rate expectations:

**Cap Rate Spread Framework:**
- Average cap rate spread over 10Y Treasury: 150-250bps (historical)
- Current spread = cheap/expensive signal for the sector
- When spread > 250bps: REITs are CHEAP relative to bonds (buy)
- When spread < 150bps: REITs are EXPENSIVE (sell or be selective)

**Key signal**: Fed pivot from hiking to cutting = most powerful REIT catalyst. REITs typically rally 15-25% in the 6 months following the last rate hike.

### Sub-Sector Ranking (Structural Tailwinds to Headwinds)

**Tier 1: Structural Demand Growth (BUY on weakness)**
1. **Data Centers (EQIX, DLR, QTS)** — AI-driven power demand is the strongest tailwind. Power constraints limit new supply. 15-20% NOI growth visibility
2. **Industrial/Logistics (PLD, REXR)** — E-commerce penetration still growing but decelerating. New supply catching up in some markets. Be selective
3. **Senior Housing (WELL, VTR)** — Demographics (baby boomers) + construction pipeline dried up during COVID = multi-year supply/demand imbalance
4. **Gaming (VICI)** — Triple-net leases to casinos. Zero operating risk, inflation-linked escalators, 4-5% yields

**Tier 2: Stable/Recovering**
5. **Net Lease (O, NNN)** — Bond proxies with inflation escalators. Rate-sensitive but defensive
6. **Self-Storage (PSA, EXR)** — Supply/demand normalizing after COVID boom. Still solid but growth decelerating
7. **Residential (EQR, AVB, MAA)** — Rent growth normalizing. Sun Belt oversupply risk, coastal supply-constrained markets holding up

**Tier 3: Structural Headwinds (Caution)**
8. **Office (VNO, SLG, BXP)** — Remote work has permanently impaired demand. BUT: Class A trophy office in gateway cities = different story than suburban commodity
9. **Retail (SPG, MAC)** — Mall traffic declining but high-quality malls (SPG A-malls) are scarce assets with redevelopment optionality

### The Data Center Thesis (Most Important Sub-Sector)

This is the single biggest structural shift in commercial real estate:

**Demand drivers:**
- AI training clusters: 50-100MW per facility
- Cloud migration continues: enterprise on-prem → colo/hyperscale
- Edge computing: 5G/IoT driving distributed facility demand
- Government/defense: data sovereignty requirements

**Supply constraints (the key to pricing power):**
- Power availability is THE bottleneck. New grid interconnection takes 3-5 years
- Zoning/permitting delays in key markets (Northern Virginia, Dallas, Phoenix)
- Construction costs rising (specialized equipment, labor)
- Result: demand growing 25-30% vs supply growing 10-15% = pricing power

**Key signal**: When hyperscalers sign multi-year pre-lease agreements at record $/MW rates = confirmed demand with long-duration visibility.

### NAV Discount/Premium Framework

REITs should trade near Net Asset Value (NAV = market value of properties - debt). Deviations are investment signals:

**Deep discount to NAV (>20%)**:
- Usually happens during rate spikes or sector-specific fear
- Opportunity if: property cash flows stable, debt maturity manageable, dividend covered
- Trap if: property values actually declining (office), high leverage with near-term maturities

**Premium to NAV (>10%)**:
- Market expects above-average growth (data centers, senior housing)
- Justified if: genuine structural demand growth + limited supply
- Dangerous if: premium from yield-chasing in low-rate environment

**Key signal**: When private market transactions occur at cap rates 100bps+ tighter than implied public REIT cap rates = public market is mispricing.

### Debt Maturity Wall Analysis

The most underappreciated risk in REITs:

- REITs that issued 3-5 year debt in 2020-2021 at 2-3% rates face refinancing at 5-6%
- Impact: 100-200bps hit to AFFO for every tranche refinanced
- Winners: REITs with long-weighted-average debt maturity (7+ years), low leverage (<6x debt/EBITDA)
- Losers: Highly levered REITs with near-term maturities (some office, some hotel)

## Earnings Call Intelligence

**What to listen for:**
1. "Same-store NOI growth" — the purest organic growth metric
2. "Occupancy" vs "economic occupancy" — gap indicates concession/free rent
3. "Leasing spreads" (cash vs GAAP) — cash spreads show real pricing power
4. "Development pipeline" — starts vs deliveries, yield on cost vs cap rate
5. "Debt maturity" — upcoming maturities and expected refinancing rate

## Risk Factors
- Duration risk: REITs behave like long-duration bonds in rate spikes
- Secular shifts: work-from-home, e-commerce are permanent structural changes
- Leverage: most REITs run 5-7x debt/EBITDA — rate sensitivity compounds with leverage
